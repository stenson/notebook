bak kut teh // meat bone soup

ròu gŭ chá

There are many variations on bak kut teh, or “meat bone tea,” but the two main camps are 1) the Teochew version, heavy on white pepper, that is often associated with Singapore, and 2) the darker Hokkien version, brewed with a range of medicinal herbs and spices, that is more popular in Malaysia. Confusingly, the Hokkienese term “bak kut teh” has come to define both versions in the Chinese diaspora of Southeast Asia (in Teochew, this dish sounds more like “neh gu deh”).

～

Bak kut teh is near infinitely customizable. This recipe is a stripped down version that really highlights the foundational flavors of the Teochew version. Don’t be scared off by the quantity of garlic and peppercorns; their bite is tamed by a long simmer. If you’d like, you can add some dried shiitake mushroom to infuse the broth, or top the finished soup with sliced scallions or chopped cilantro. As for me, I find beauty in the simplicity of this recipe. With so few ingredients, the details are important: salting the ribs ahead of time ensures a tender texture and better flavor; toasting the white peppercorns rounds out the sharper, more pungent notes; and initially blanching the ribs results in a cleaner broth.

2 lbs pork ribs

50 g whole white peppercorns (about 5 tbsp)

6 garlic bulbs

Salt

Thai bird’s eye chili

Soy sauce

Cut ribs into individual segments.

Liberally salt each piece, as if you were salting a chicken to roast.

Let salted ribs sit in the refrigerator for at least a few hours and up to two days before cooking.

~ When you’re ready to cook: ~

Set water to boil in a stockpot.

Toast white peppercorns in a dry pan or wok over medium-low heat, swirling for a few minutes, until fragrant and just starting to tan.

Crush peppercorns, in mortar & pestle or with a heavy pot. The more you crush, the stronger the pepper flavor.

Separate garlic bulbs into cloves, but do not peel. Rinse skin-on garlic cloves.

For easier serving, tie up peppercorns and garlic gloves inside a cheesecloth or muslin bundle.

When water is boiling, add ribs and bring back up to a boil. Continue boiling for 5 minutes to remove impurities from bones and meat.

After 5 minutes, remove ribs and discard water.

If there are visible impurities (like blood spots) still on the ribs, give them a rinse.

Add ribs back to the stockpot and add just enough water to cover.

Add garlic and peppercorns and bring to a boil.

Once boiling, reduce heat to low and let simmer, covered, for about an hour until the ribs are tender. Taste for texture and continue simmering if needed.

Stir in 1 tsp of salt and taste. Add more salt to taste.

Slice Thai bird’s eye chili into soy sauce for a simple dipping sauce.

Serve with hot rice, a small dish of dipping sauce, and a vegetable side dish for a complete meal.

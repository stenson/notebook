金不换海瓜子

stir-fried basil clams

jīnbùhuàn hǎi guāzǐ

1.5 tbsp cooking oil

1.5-2 lbs small clams, scrubbed*

3 cloves garlic, minced

2 sprigs Thai basil, divided into sections

5 Thai basil leaves, slivered

1 hot chili pepper, sliced**

1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine

*Select the smallest clams available to you. Manila clams are widely available and work well, with sweet & tender meat. New Zealand cockles are a great (briny) alternative.

**Use your preferred pepper, such as jalapeño for a mild spice, or Thai bird’s eye chili for more serious heat.

Heat wok on high. When hot (sprinkle a few beads of water to test — they should vaporize immediately), swirl in cooking oil.

Add minced garlic and stir-fry for a few seconds until fragrant.

Add clams (there will be oil splatter — have splatter guard on hand!). Stir-fry to coat clams in garlic-fragrant oil.

Add Shaoxing rice wine, basil, and chili pepper slices. Stir-fry to distribute and cover wok. Let clams simmer a minute.

Check how clams look. Stir-toss to redistribute clams in wok. Cover again.

Check on clams’ progress about every minute, stir-tossing each time. When most clams have opened (if clams are small, may be in as little as 3 minutes), transfer to serving bowl.

Sprinkle slivered thai basil on top and serve.
